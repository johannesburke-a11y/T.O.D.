Root Causes

Three reasons AI change breaks down.

Fear replaces curiosity
Without clear narrative, people default to anxiety about job security and irrelevance — paralysing adoption before it starts.
Leaders go silent at the critical moment
Ambiguity from the top is interpreted as bad news. Consistent, visible sponsorship is non-negotiable in an AI transition.
Change lands in isolation
AI tools are deployed team by team with no shared language, no common upskilling path, and no connective tissue across the org.

People-Centred Design

Meet people where they actually are.

Sceptics
Show, don't tell
Demonstrate quick wins in their domain. Evidence beats persuasion every time.
Early Adopters
Make them champions
Give them visibility, resources, and a mandate to share what they've learned.
Anxious Majority
Acknowledge the uncertainty
Don't dismiss fear. Name it, address it, and give people agency in the transition.
Senior Leaders
Lead by doing
Visible use of AI tools by leadership signals safety and normalises adoption.

Leadership Shift

From directing change to modelling it.

❌ Old Approach
Announce AI strategy in an all-hands and move on
Delegate rollout to IT and training teams
Measure tool adoption rates as a proxy for success
Stay silent when concerns are raised
✅ New Approach
Return to the narrative repeatedly — every forum, every week
Co-own the change with HR, teams, and individual managers
Measure behaviour change, confidence, and output quality
Name resistance openly and make space for honest dialogue
